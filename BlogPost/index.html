La Tía Juana

In 1829, 9 years after the consolidation of Independence, José María Echeandía, governor of the Californias, granted Santiago Argüello Moraga an area of six large livestock sites, equivalent to 10,000 hectares, which would be called "Tia Juana Ranch". On July 11, 1889, the agreement was signed and concluded the dispute that Don Santiago Argüello sustained for a long time over the land of “Rancho Tía Juana”. The city was founded on July 11, 1889 as a result of urban development. Tijuana is a Mexican city located in the state of Baja California, is the corner of the country. It's the 2nd most crowded city in Mexico with a total of 1,810,645 inhabitants in the year 2020. It is classified as a global city for cultural and commercial center, as well as a dominant production center in North America.
